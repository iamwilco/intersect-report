# Secretary Lloyd Duhon - Influence Analysis

**Unofficial Research | Based on CBC Meeting Summaries (Oct 2025 - Mar 2026)**
**Status:** Preliminary - Summaries only, no transcripts available
**Date:** April 9, 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Lloyd Duhon holds the title of "Budget Secretary" on the Cardano Budget Committee. An analysis of all 18 available meeting summaries (October 6, 2025 through March 16, 2026) reveals that Lloyd consistently operates **far beyond the scope of a traditional secretary role**. He functions as the committee's de facto operational leader: opening and closing meetings, setting agendas, directing strategy, drafting key documents, making policy proposals, managing communications, and coordinating with the Intersect Board - all while the elected Chair (first Kristijan Kowalsky, later Megan Hess) is present.

This analysis documents specific instances where Lloyd's actions exceed secretarial duties, organized by category.

---

## 2. What a Secretary Should Do

Per the [Budget Committee Charter](https://budgetcommittee.docs.intersectmbo.org/standards-operational-procedures/page), the Secretary's formal duties include:

- Recording tallies and actions during votes
- Organizing exercises for prioritization and RACI definitions
- Synthesizing feedback and building reports
- Coordinating public communications from committee members
- Drafting surrounding text and supporting materials for governance actions
- Managing information flows (ClickUp, GitHub, etc.)

A secretary is an **administrative support role**, not a leadership or policy-making position. The secretary does not vote.

## 10. Conclusion

Based on 18 meeting summaries, Lloyd Duhon does **not** operate as a neutral secretary. He operates as the committee's de facto leader. Specifically:

1. **Meeting Control:** Opens, runs, and closes every meeting - the Chair's role
2. **Policy Origination:** Proposes NCL strategies, budget frameworks, treasury management structures, and technical architectures
3. **Board Communication:** Directly interfaces with the Intersect Board on substantive decisions
4. **Narrative Control:** Authors the committee's public communications, blogs, videos, and social media strategy
5. **Cross-Committee Influence:** Attends other committee meetings, holds simultaneous positions across 8+ Intersect bodies
6. **Financial Interest:** Holds paid contracts for work he helps define in his secretary role
7. **Tooling Ownership:** Personally developing budget infrastructure and seeking funding for his own prior development work

### Is this necessarily problematic?

Not automatically. Lloyd appears to be competent and deeply committed. The committee might be more effective because of his leadership. However, this concentration of operational power in an unelected administrative role raises governance concerns:

- **Accountability gap:** A secretary is not elected by DReps and cannot be removed by community vote the way committee members can
- **Structural opacity:** External observers would expect the Chair to be the committee's leader; the reality is different from the org chart
- **Conflict of interest:** Multiple simultaneous roles across governance bodies, combined with paid contracts, create structural conflicts even if individual decisions are made in good faith

## 11. Limitations

- **No transcripts available.** Summaries may compress, omit, or reframe contributions. A speaker attributed as "proposing" something in a summary may have been relaying committee consensus.
- **Summary authorship unknown.** If Lloyd (or someone working with him) produced the summaries, his contributions may be over-represented.
- **Chair delegation possible.** The Chair may have explicitly asked Lloyd to run meetings. This would change the governance interpretation but not the structural observation.
- **Summaries cover Oct 2025 - Mar 2026 only.** Earlier periods may show different dynamics.
