### Recommendations for Immediate Implementation

1. **Formalize Role Descriptions**
   - Create written role boundaries for Chair, Vice Chair, and Secretary
   - Require acknowledgment from role holders
   - Include in committee operating procedures

2. **Implement Structured Decision-Making**
   - Formal motions and seconds required
   - Recorded votes on all decisions
   - Clear quorum requirements enforced

3. **Fix Role Boundary Violations**
   - Secretary limited to: minutes, logistics, document management
   - No substantive agenda additions without Chair approval
   - No governance participation beyond administration

4. **Strengthen Leadership Continuity**
   - Mandatory overlap period for transitions
   - Written handover documentation required
   - "Shadow" period for incoming leaders

5. **Improve Meeting Structure**
   - Pre-published agendas with time allocations
   - Formal opening and closing by Chair
   - Action items with assigned owners and deadlines

### Conclusion

The GMC demonstrates strong strategic thinking and ecosystem understanding, particularly through Tim Harrison's leadership. However, operational execution suffers from unclear boundaries, informal procedures, and transition gaps. The committee operates more as a collaborative working group than a formal governance body, which may limit its effectiveness in oversight and decision-making.

The upcoming April 2026 election cycle presents an opportunity to address these structural issues through formal role definitions, improved procedures, and stronger accountability mechanisms.
