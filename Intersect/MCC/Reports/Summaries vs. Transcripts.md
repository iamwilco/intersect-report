## Overall Patterns Across All 11 Meetings

Having read every word of every transcript and summary, these are the consistent patterns:

**1. Tone laundering.** The summaries systematically remove anger, frustration, and conflict and replace them with neutral procedural language. Maureen calling the ClickUp board "b***shit" becomes "Maureen expressed frustration." A 20-minute confrontation about meeting access becomes "Terence offered continued support." The rug incident becomes "Abhik denied closing the meeting intentionally."

**2. Proposed ideas become decisions.** Ideas floated by Maureen or others — especially things she said "I was thinking we could..." or "what do you guys think about..." — are repeatedly written up as committee decisions. The event reimbursement model, the contribution records being made public, the bi-weekly meeting schedule, the handbook exploration — none of these were voted on, yet all appear as decisions.

**3. Invented action items.** Numerous action items in the summaries were never stated in the meetings. The most consistent offender is the pattern where Abhik assigns himself an action item that he mentioned in passing or was implied, then records it formally. Examples: "Knowledge Base Review: The committee members are to review the existing 'Recent Updates' section" (Dec 16), "Formal request for Hub data" (Mar 17), several ClickUp-related tasks.

**4. Sanitizing accountability failures.** The summaries consistently omit or minimize evidence of committee dysfunction: Nathan's continued absence is mentioned only in one sentence across 11 summaries despite being a persistent issue; the WhatsApp/silo problem (which Wilco and Laurentine raised repeatedly) is almost never captured; ClickUp access failures that were a running complaint are usually reduced to "ClickUp training will be arranged."

**5. The Dec 9 / Dec 16 confusion is now fixed.** The previous Dec 9 summary was using the wrong content (Dec 16 content). With the corrected file, Dec 9 now correctly summarizes the workshop follow-up meeting. However, note that the Dec 9 summary still has the issue noted above regarding the "twice weekly" office hours decision.

**6. Attendance table inaccuracies.** Several summaries mark all members as "Yes" for attendance when transcripts clearly show otherwise. The Jan 20 summary marks everyone present (10/11 members) — the transcript shows Akheel joined late and several others had audio issues, but four members are actually absent per the attendees list in the transcript itself.

In short: your instinct is correct. The summaries are not neutral records. They are an edited, polished version of events that removes conflict, promotes informal suggestions to formal decisions, and in several cases adds action items that were never stated in the meeting.
