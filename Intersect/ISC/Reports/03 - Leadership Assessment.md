### Overall Leadership Assessment

**Is the committee well-led?**
The committee functions adequately under its collegial model but shows signs of needing more structure. The chair-less design works when all members are engaged, but meetings sometimes lack clear facilitation, leading to:
- Deferred agenda items
- Extended discussions without resolution
- Inconsistent follow-through on actions

**Biggest Leadership Failure:**
The most significant failure was the delayed transparency around the Executive Director appointment process. Despite ISC members participating in the selection panel, they learned of the decision through external channels, undermining trust: "Leadership acknowledged the point and committed to provide clarity on announcement timing within 24 hours" (20260112).

**Recommended Changes:**
1. **Introduce a rotating facilitator role** - While maintaining the collegial structure, designate a facilitator for each meeting to ensure agenda completion
2. **Strengthen action tracking** - More explicit documentation of decisions, owners, and deadlines
3. **Establish time limits** - Enforce time boxes for agenda items to prevent chronic deferrals
4. **Improve executive-committee communication protocols** - Clear expectations for when ISC should be informed of major decisions

The collegial model has merit but requires more operational discipline to function effectively. The committee achieves strategic outcomes but could benefit from tactical improvements in meeting management and follow-through.
