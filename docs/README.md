## Reports

### Report 01 — Summaries vs. Transcripts
A meeting-by-meeting comparison finding that the official summaries systematically: remove conflict and frustration (tone laundering), convert proposed ideas into formal decisions, invent action items that were never discussed, and omit accountability failures.

### Report 02 — Member Participation
Attendance records and qualitative analysis for all 10 elected members. Key findings: Nathan Kanapi attended 0 of 11 meetings (seat effectively vacant for 5+ months), Ian McCullough attended only 6/11 (55%), Olivier Rishi contributed almost nothing verbally across 9 meetings. Laurentine Djatsa and Boaz Balume completed the French site translation — a genuine deliverable largely unrecognised in official records.

### Report 03 — The Secretaries
Critical examination of Secretary Abhik Nag (minutes chronically late, ClickUp access failures unresolved for 5 months, systematically overreaches into governance) and former Secretary Terence McCutcheon (attends 9/11 meetings with no formal role, uses institutional memory to frame what the committee is allowed to want, holds calendar ownership as structural leverage).

## Methodology

All reports are based on a complete reading of the official AI-generated meeting transcripts and the official meeting summaries (minutes) for all 11 MCC meetings. Every factual claim is traceable to a specific transcript passage.

These reports represent independent community analysis and are not affiliated with Intersect or any official body. All source recordings and transcripts are publicly linked via Intersect's official documentation channels.
