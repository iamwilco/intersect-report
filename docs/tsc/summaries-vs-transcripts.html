Report 01

Summaries vs. Transcripts

A forensic, meeting-by-meeting comparison of the official summaries against the raw transcripts for all 23 TSC meetings. Every meeting was fully read in both formats. 95% of meetings contained fabricated or misrepresented decisions. 85% had attendance record errors. Conflict and criticism were systematically sanitized.

Overall Finding

The summaries cannot be relied upon as factual accounts. They systematically inflate informal discussions into formal "decisions," fabricate attendance records including a phantom "TBC" Vice Chair, erase all substantive conflict and criticism, and strip technical complexity from the record. They benefit Intersect leadership and the status quo while harming accountability, institutional memory, and the Cardano community.

Patterns Across All 23 Meetings

Four consistent patterns emerge from the full comparison:

  • Decision inflation and authority laundering: 19 of 20 meetings analysed (95%) contained at least one fabricated or misrepresented decision. Suggestions from individuals are consistently presented as committee decisions, with words like "agreed," "decided," or "confirmed" applied where no vote occurred.
  • Attendance record fabrication: 17 of 20 meetings (85%) contained attendance errors. People listed as present who never spoke, specific absences claimed without evidence, and a phantom "TBC" Vice Chair role appearing in multiple summaries despite no such person existing.
  • Conflict and criticism sanitization: Every instance of substantive disagreement is erased. Neil Davies' statements about the TSC being "emasculated," his censure of the board, Nicolas Biri calling discussions "stupid," and Neil's "$150 is bloody laughable" — all systematically removed.
  • Technical complexity reduction: Critical warnings about Ouroboros-Leios unpredictability, node memory leaks, 150GB chain growth, and constitutional security vulnerabilities are routinely simplified or omitted entirely.

Methodology

Every meeting was read in full in both formats: the official summary and the raw transcript. Claims in the summary were cross-referenced line by line against the transcript. The three categories used — Fabricated/Misrepresented, Omitted, and Broadly Accurate — reflect the degree of divergence from what was actually said. Direct quotes from transcripts are provided where they illustrate the gap most clearly.
